Un-Frequently Asked Questions

Some questions I've been turning my head around, without finding an appropriate answer. At some point I will try to phrase some proper answers. For the moment it's just a sketch of things I appreciate input on.

Does power corrupt?

My sensation is that yes, it does. This would imply that people with greater power should be particularly careful of not abusing it (which is not the same a the quote from Spiderman).

Questions I'm able to answer

Are rich people smarter?

No, for god's sake. Even though this should be self-evident (except, apparently, those whose agenda is helped by this kind of retorics), there is a nice paper by Levy (sadly it is published for profit, but at least it's not behind a paywall) showing that the ability of the richest can be upper-bounded by a low number.

Are some genders/collectives smarter than others?

This kind of question begs for a "yes", so that people can incorporate their concept of superiority into societal oppression. There is no reasonable evidence in favor of this kind of statement, though there is a ton of material showing how discriminated collectives such as women or African Americans suffer from systematic biases that make their lives much harder. If any such kind of statement needs to be done, it is that northwestern white cis males should by now all be semi-gods given the amount of privilege they enjoyed, and given they are not, perhaps we should start doubting about their skills. Of course, the real answer is that our world is complex and cannot be trivialized by any kind of such statements.
