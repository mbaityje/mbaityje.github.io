Internships

Can I join your group for an internship?

If after surfing my whole website you are still motivated in doing your project in my group, then you are likely welcome to join, provided that I have a suitable research project for you and that I have time to supervise you. This changes along the year, because I do not always have enough time to supervise students, and because some projects might not be available anymore. Also, although I often have projects that are suitable for a masters thesis, the subjects vary and some may not suit your background.
Important: Due to updated migration laws, I am now only able to hire non-Swiss interns if the internship is a compulsory part of their study. If the migration office does not have an official document stating this, they will not issue the visa.

Which kind of background should I have to join your group?

This depends a lot on the project and should be taken with a grain of salt. For the more theoretical projects, mathematics, theoretical physics or computer science. For the applied ones, data science, statistics and environmental science/engineering.

What kind of project could I do in your group?

This highly depends on when you apply. Topics may statistical physics of disordered systems (dynamics of REM and p-spin model), statistical physics of species community models, machine learning theory (training dynamics and data imbalance), machine learning plankton classification and forecasting, toxicity prediction (applying methods to predict how toxic chemicals are on aquatic species), and detection of mining assets.

Will AI steal my job?

No. Your job will be stolen, and AI will be blamed for it, but the culprit is social inequality. AI could also mean that we all maintain our job while working less and making the same money, having more time for our children, for our hobbies, and for constructing the social tissue which is fundamental in a true democracy. To blame AI is to tacitly accept the social injustice that is present in our society, with or without AI. I wrote a short article on Towards Data Science on this topic, which you can find here.
