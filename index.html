Marco Baity-Jesi (he/him)

I am a theoretical physicist, Group Leader at Eawag, a research institute of the ETH domain (Switzerland), where lead the Machine Learning and Complex Systems Group.

Research interests

  • Theory of machine learning. How biases in data and architecture can influence AI.
  • Machine learning applied to ecological risk assessment. Using AI as an alternative to animal testing.
  • Theory of complexity applied to ecology. Emergent behaviors in ecological communities with a large number of species.
  • Machine learning applied to ecology monitoring. AI software for automated biodiversity monitoring.
  • Machine learning applied to ecological forecasting. Prediction of harmful algal blooms.

Further (not less important) interests

  • Grassroots community organizing. Tango Rebelde @ Practica Maravillas.
  • Scientific political advocacy. Debunking the narrative of Science being apolitical. Adhesion to Science for the People, public talks.
  • Antimilitarism. IUS and events.

My professional interests

I am a Group Leader at Eawag, a research institute of the ETH domain (Switzerland). In my group, we use methods from machine learning and statistical physics to approach problems related to water quality and ecology. In addition, we are interested in understanding the dynamics of high-dimensional systems (such as deep neural networks, glasses or ecological networks) in the presence of a large number of constraints.
