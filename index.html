Marco BAITY JESI (site under construction)

I am a computational physicist, interested in statistical physics and hard optimization problems. My broad research topic is rugged landscapes, intended as high-dimensional systems that often have a large number of solutions separated by barriers. I investigate the statistical properties of such systems, and how the dynamics takes place. Throughout my carrier, I have been focused on

  • Learning dynamics of Deep Neural Networks
  • Activated dynamics in high-dimensional toy models
  • Applying method from machine learning and statistical physics to ecology
  • Dynamics of supercooled liquids
  • Energy landscape, phase transitions and dynamics in spin glasses
  • Jamming of sphere packings

Currently, I am a research associate at Columbia University in the City of New York, USA. Before this, I was at CEA Saclay in France, at the Universidad Complutense de Madrid in Spain, and at Sapienza, University of Rome in Italy.
