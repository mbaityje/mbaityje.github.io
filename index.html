Elevator pitch

I am a Group Leader at Eawag (Switzerland). With my group, I use methods from machine learning and statistical physics to approach problems mainly related to water systems. In addition, I am interested in understanding the dynamics of high-dimensional systems (such as deep neural networks or glasses) in the presence of a large number of constraints. In addition to my research topics, I have a personal concern about how science, as an instrument of power and knowledge, can be used both to improve our lives and to perpetrate injustice.

Career path

Currently, I am a research scientist at Columbia University in the City of New York, USA. Before this, I was at CEA Saclay in France, at the Universidad Complutense de Madrid in Spain, and at Sapienza, University of Rome in Italy.
