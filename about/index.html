Biography

I am a theoretical physicist and the leader of the Machine Learning & Complex Systems Group at Eawag, the Swiss Federal Institute of Aquatic Science and Technology.

My work bridges theory and application of machine learning. On the theoretical side, I investigate how data and model choices shape the behavior and biases of AI systems. On the applied side, I work on environmental challenges such as monitoring aquatic ecosystems and assessing ecological risk.

Beyond research, I am interested in the social role of science, its impact on labor and on human rights.
